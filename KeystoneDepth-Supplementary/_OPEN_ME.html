Comparison with Stereo Magnification

Here, we provide a comparison with stereo magnification, the most related approach, using code generously obtained from the authors. Our method performs better on most examples. Here we show some representative examples. Please pay attention to the disoccluded region when observing these results. In many other cases, both methods provide less than satisfactory results, with different types of failures. In the last example, for instance, we produce holes, which stereo magnification avoids, but inserts stretchy artifacts instead. One disadvantage of our approach is that it can produce jagged edges, whereas the Multiplane Image (MPI) representation of stereo magnification provides a natural antialiasing.
